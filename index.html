Summary Of: 9 Projects I’m Working On That Makes Me Forget I Own a phone

By Candice

ONE SENTENCE SUMMARY:

The author shares nine personal projects, like deep reading and creating, that help them replace phone scrolling with engaging activities.

MAIN POINTS:

  1. The author describes a past over-reliance on their phone for validation and entertainment.
  2. They advocate for finding engaging projects to avoid mindless scrolling.
  3. One project is a deep analysis of Dostoyevsky's "White Nights."
  4. Another involves rereading and analyzing Murakami's "The City and Its Uncertain Walls."
  5. Writing a weekly Substack newsletter helps them mindfully consume media.
  6. They are creating an aggressively pink personal website for fun.
  7. Literary bullet journaling brings joy through creative book summaries.
  8. The author pursues research on topics like moral absolutism for personal growth.
  9. Growing a digital garden helps organize thoughts and online discoveries.
  10. Regular exercise and managing a YouTube channel also fill their time.

TAKEAWAYS:

  1. Replacing phone addiction requires proactive engagement in fulfilling personal projects.
  2. Deep engagement with literature, through rereading and analysis, can be a rich hobby.
  3. Creating content, like newsletters or websites, fosters mindful consumption and self-expression.
  4. Journaling can be a creative outlet for hobbies like reading.
  5. Intentional activities and hobbies can make one "too busy to scroll."
